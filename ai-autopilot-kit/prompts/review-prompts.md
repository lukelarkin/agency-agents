# Weekly Review Prompt Chains

> 6 copy-paste prompt chains for System 4: The CEO Weekly Review Dashboard.
> Each prompt has [BRACKETS] where you fill in your specific information.

---

## Prompt Chain #1: CEO Scorecard Builder

> **Used in:** System 4, Step 1 (One-time setup)
> **Purpose:** Creates your personalized weekly scorecard — the numbers that actually matter

```
Help me build a weekly CEO scorecard for my business. I want 8–12 key metrics that tell me if my business is healthy.

**My business:**
- Type: [YOUR BUSINESS TYPE — e.g., marketing agency, web design studio, consulting firm]
- Business model: [PROJECT-BASED / RETAINER / PRODUCTIZED / MIX]
- Revenue range: $[X]–$[Y] per month
- Team size: [JUST ME / 2–5 / 5+]
- Main revenue sources: [LIST YOUR REVENUE STREAMS]

**My goals for the next 90 days:**
1. [GOAL 1 — be specific and measurable]
2. [GOAL 2]
3. [GOAL 3]

**What I consider a "good week":**
[DESCRIBE WHAT A GREAT WEEK LOOKS AND FEELS LIKE]

**What I consider a "bad week":**
[DESCRIBE WHAT A BAD WEEK LOOKS AND FEELS LIKE]

Build my scorecard with:

1. **8–12 metrics** organized into these categories:
   - Revenue & Cash (2–3 metrics)
   - Pipeline & Sales (2–3 metrics)
   - Marketing & Leads (2–3 metrics)
   - Client Health (1–2 metrics)
   - Operations & Capacity (1–2 metrics)

2. For each metric, provide:
   - The metric name
   - How to measure it (where to find the number)
   - A target/benchmark to aim for
   - Whether it's a leading indicator (predicts future) or lagging (measures past)

3. **A one-page scorecard template** I can fill in every Friday

4. **Warning signs:** What combinations of numbers should trigger alarm bells
   (e.g., "If leads drop 2 weeks in a row while hours worked stays high, you have a marketing problem")

Focus on numbers that actually drive decisions, not vanity metrics. I'd rather track 8 meaningful numbers than 20 feel-good ones.
```

## Prompt Chain #2: Weekly Review Analyzer

> **Used in:** System 4, Step 2 (Every Friday)
> **Purpose:** Analyzes your weekly numbers and gives actionable insights

```
Analyze my weekly business performance and give me actionable insights.

**Week of:** [DATE]

**My numbers this week:**
- Revenue collected: $[AMOUNT]
- Revenue invoiced: $[AMOUNT]
- New leads/inquiries: [NUMBER]
- Discovery calls: [NUMBER]
- Proposals sent: [NUMBER]
- Proposals won: [NUMBER]
- Proposals lost/expired: [NUMBER]
- Content pieces published: [NUMBER]
- Email open rate: [PERCENTAGE]
- Active clients: [NUMBER]
- Hours worked: [NUMBER]
- Billable hours: [NUMBER]
- Cash in bank: $[AMOUNT]

**Last week's numbers (for comparison):**
[PASTE LAST WEEK'S KEY NUMBERS — or say "first week, no comparison"]

**4-week average (if you have it):**
[PASTE AVERAGES — or say "not enough data yet"]

**Notable events this week:**
- [ANYTHING UNUSUAL — lost a client, big win, launched something, got sick, etc.]

Analyze and provide:

1. **Performance Summary:** Up, down, or flat vs. last week and 4-week average. One paragraph.

2. **3 Wins to Celebrate:**
   - Even small ones. What went right? What progress was made?

3. **3 Concerns to Address:**
   - What's declining, missing, or at risk?
   - For each concern: suggested action + urgency level (handle now, plan for next week, or monitor)

4. **3 Priorities for Next Week:**
   - Specific, actionable, time-bound
   - Connected to the concerns or goals above
   - Each one should be completable in 2–4 hours

5. **One Big Insight:**
   - Something I might not have noticed. A pattern, a correlation, or a strategic observation.

Be direct. Don't sugarcoat problems. I need honesty, not encouragement.
```

## Prompt Chain #3: Decision Clarifier

> **Used in:** System 4, Step 3 (As needed)
> **Purpose:** Walks you through a tough business decision with structure

```
Help me think through a business decision I need to make.

**The decision:** [DESCRIBE THE DECISION IN ONE SENTENCE]

**Context:**
[PROVIDE RELEVANT BACKGROUND — what led to this decision point]

**Data I have:**
[ANY NUMBERS, FACTS, OR EVIDENCE RELEVANT TO THE DECISION]

**My gut feeling:**
[WHAT DOES YOUR INSTINCT SAY — and why might it be wrong]

Walk me through this decision:

1. **Clarify the decision:** What's the actual choice I'm making? (Often the real decision is different from what we think it is)

2. **Options:** List all realistic options (including "do nothing")

3. **For each option, analyze:**
   - Best case outcome
   - Worst case outcome
   - Most likely outcome
   - Reversibility (how easy is it to undo?)
   - Time to see results

4. **The "friend test":** If a trusted friend described this exact situation, what would I advise them?

5. **10/10/10 test:** How will I feel about this decision in 10 minutes? 10 months? 10 years?

6. **Recommendation:** Based on this analysis, which option and why?

7. **First action step:** If I go with your recommendation, what's the very first thing I should do? (Make it small and doable today)

Don't just validate my gut feeling. Challenge it if the data suggests otherwise.
```

## Prompt Chain #4: Weekly Planning Generator

> **Used in:** System 4, Step 4 (Every Friday)
> **Purpose:** Creates a time-blocked plan for the upcoming week

```
Create a focused weekly plan based on my priorities and calendar.

**My 3 priorities for next week:**
1. [PRIORITY 1 — from your weekly review]
2. [PRIORITY 2]
3. [PRIORITY 3]

**My calendar constraints:**
- Client meetings already scheduled: [LIST THEM]
- Recurring commitments: [WEEKLY CALLS, TEAM MEETINGS, ETC.]
- Days/times I'm unavailable: [ANY BLOCKS]
- My most productive time: [MORNING / AFTERNOON / EVENING]

**My typical weekly structure:**
- Content creation day: [WHICH DAY — usually Monday]
- Client work days: [WHICH DAYS]
- Admin/CEO day: [WHICH DAY — usually Friday]
- Follow-up block: [WHEN DO YOU DO YOUR DAILY FOLLOW-UP]

**Hours available for priority work:** ~[NUMBER] hours

Create:

1. **Time-blocked weekly plan:**
   For each day (Monday–Friday), show:
   - Specific time blocks for priority work
   - Client work blocks
   - Admin/email blocks (batched, not throughout the day)
   - Buffer time for unexpected items (at least 20% of each day)

2. **Priority breakdown:**
   For each priority, specify:
   - Exactly when I'll work on it
   - What "done" looks like
   - First action to start with (so I don't waste 15 min figuring out where to begin)

3. **Non-negotiables:**
   - 3 things that MUST happen this week (even if everything else falls apart)

4. **"If things go sideways" plan:**
   - If I only get 50% of my planned time, which tasks survive?

Keep it realistic. I'd rather finish 3 things than half-finish 7.
```

## Prompt Chain #5: Monthly Trend Analyzer

> **Used in:** System 4, Step 5 (Last Friday of the month)
> **Purpose:** Analyzes 4 weeks of data to reveal trends and patterns

```
Analyze my business performance over the past 4 weeks and identify trends.

**Week 1 (date: [DATE]):**
Revenue: $[X] | Leads: [X] | Proposals sent: [X] | Won: [X] | Content: [X] | Hours: [X] | Energy: [X]/10

**Week 2 (date: [DATE]):**
Revenue: $[X] | Leads: [X] | Proposals sent: [X] | Won: [X] | Content: [X] | Hours: [X] | Energy: [X]/10

**Week 3 (date: [DATE]):**
Revenue: $[X] | Leads: [X] | Proposals sent: [X] | Won: [X] | Content: [X] | Hours: [X] | Energy: [X]/10

**Week 4 (date: [DATE]):**
Revenue: $[X] | Leads: [X] | Proposals sent: [X] | Won: [X] | Content: [X] | Hours: [X] | Energy: [X]/10

**My 90-day goals:**
1. [GOAL 1] — target: [SPECIFIC NUMBER/OUTCOME]
2. [GOAL 2] — target: [SPECIFIC NUMBER/OUTCOME]
3. [GOAL 3] — target: [SPECIFIC NUMBER/OUTCOME]

Analyze and provide:

1. **Trend Report:**
   - Which metrics are improving, declining, or flat?
   - What's the trajectory? (If current trends continue, where will I be in 90 days?)

2. **Correlations:**
   - Any relationships between metrics? (e.g., "Weeks with 5+ content pieces had 2x more leads")
   - Leading indicators: What early numbers predict later results?

3. **90-Day Goal Check:**
   - Am I on track for each goal? Show the math.
   - If not on track, what needs to change and by when?

4. **Energy & Sustainability:**
   - Is my energy trending up or down? Is there a correlation with hours worked?
   - Am I working sustainably or heading toward burnout?

5. **Strategic Recommendations:**
   - One thing to START doing next month
   - One thing to STOP doing next month
   - One thing to DOUBLE DOWN on

6. **Updated Focus Areas:**
   - Based on these trends, what should my top 3 focus areas be for next month?

Be specific with numbers and percentages. "Improving" isn't helpful — "up 23% over 4 weeks" is.
```

## Prompt Chain #6: Quarterly Business Review

> **Purpose:** Comprehensive 90-day analysis for strategic planning

```
I want to do a comprehensive quarterly business review.

**This quarter's data:**

Month 1:
- Revenue: $[TOTAL]
- New clients: [NUMBER]
- Clients lost: [NUMBER]
- Proposals sent: [X], Won: [X], Lost: [X]
- Content pieces published: [NUMBER]
- Biggest win: [DESCRIBE]
- Biggest challenge: [DESCRIBE]

Month 2:
[SAME FORMAT]

Month 3:
[SAME FORMAT]

**Last quarter's total revenue (for comparison):** $[AMOUNT]
**90-day goals set at the start of this quarter:** [LIST THEM WITH TARGETS]

Conduct a full quarterly review:

1. **Quarter Summary:** 3-sentence overview of how the quarter went

2. **Goal Scorecard:** For each goal — did I hit it? By how much over/under? Why?

3. **Revenue Analysis:**
   - Monthly trajectory (growing, shrinking, flat?)
   - Revenue per client trend
   - Revenue per hour trend
   - Best revenue source

4. **Client Analysis:**
   - Net client change (new minus lost)
   - Client retention rate
   - Warning signs in current client base

5. **Marketing & Sales Analysis:**
   - Lead generation effectiveness
   - Proposal win rate trend
   - Best-performing content/channels

6. **What Worked:** Top 3 decisions/actions that moved the needle

7. **What Didn't Work:** Top 3 things that underperformed or were a waste of time

8. **Next Quarter Goals:**
   - Suggest 3 specific, measurable 90-day goals based on this data
   - For each goal: why this goal, what target, and what needs to change to hit it

9. **One Bold Move:**
   - Based on the data, what's one bigger strategic move I should consider?
   (Raise prices? Drop a service? Hire? Niche down? Launch something?)
```
