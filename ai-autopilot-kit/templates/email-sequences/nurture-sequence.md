# Lead Nurture Email Sequence

> 7-email sequence for warm leads who showed interest but went quiet. Space emails 3–5 days apart. Use Prompt Chain #3 from `prompts/follow-up-prompts.md` to personalize each email for specific leads.

---

## Email 1: The Value-Add (Day 1)

**Subject:** [Resource] that might help with [their problem]

---

Hi [First Name],

I was thinking about [the problem they mentioned] and came across something that might be useful for you.

[Share a specific resource: article, tool, framework, or tip related to their situation. Make it genuinely helpful, not a pitch.]

No strings attached — just thought of you when I saw it.

If you'd like to chat about how this applies to your situation, I'm around.

[Your Name]

---

## Email 2: The Insight (Day 4)

**Subject:** Something I noticed about [their industry/situation]

---

Hi [First Name],

I work with a lot of [their industry/role] and I've noticed a pattern that might be relevant to you:

[Share a genuine insight, trend, or observation. Something like: "Most [their role] I talk to are spending 10+ hours/week on X when they could automate 80% of it."]

Not sure if that resonates with your situation, but if it does, I have a few ideas on how to tackle it.

Worth a quick conversation?

[Your Name]

---

## Email 3: The Case Study (Day 8)

**Subject:** How [similar client] solved [their problem]

---

Hi [First Name],

Wanted to share a quick story that reminded me of your situation.

**The problem:** [Similar client] was dealing with [same problem the lead has]. They were [specific struggle — hours wasted, revenue lost, stress caused].

**What we did:** [Brief description of your solution — 2–3 sentences max]

**The result:** [Specific, measurable outcome. Numbers are powerful.]

I mention this because your situation with [reference their specific problem] sounds really similar.

If you'd like to explore whether we could get similar results for you, I'd love to chat. No pitch — just an honest conversation about whether it's a fit.

[Your Name]

---

## Email 4: The Quick Tip (Day 12)

**Subject:** Quick tip for [their problem] (takes 5 min)

---

Hi [First Name],

Here's something you can try today that might help with [their problem]:

**The tip:** [Give a genuinely actionable tip they can implement in 5 minutes. Make it good enough that it works even if they never hire you.]

**Why it works:** [One sentence explaining the principle behind it]

**Pro tip:** [An extra detail that shows your expertise]

This is one small piece of what we do for our clients. If you ever want the full system, you know where to find me.

[Your Name]

---

## Email 5: The Honest Check-In (Day 17)

**Subject:** Quick question, [First Name]

---

Hi [First Name],

I've sent you a few resources over the past couple weeks — hope they've been useful.

I wanted to ask directly: is [solving their problem] still a priority for you right now?

If yes — let's find 15 minutes to talk about it. I have some ideas specific to your situation.

If the timing isn't right — totally understand. Just let me know and I'll check back in a few months.

Either way, no hard feelings. I'd rather know than guess.

[Your Name]

---

## Email 6: The Social Proof (Day 22)

**Subject:** [Result number] in [timeframe] — here's how

---

Hi [First Name],

Quick update from our world: we just helped a client [achieve specific result] in [timeframe].

The interesting part? They started in a similar spot to where you described being — [reference their situation].

The key was [one sentence about your approach/methodology].

I share this not to brag, but because I genuinely think we could help you get similar results.

If you're interested, I have one opening in my schedule for [month]. Worth a conversation?

[Your Name]

---

## Email 7: The Graceful Close (Day 28)

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

---

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times about [their problem] and haven't heard back, so I want to be respectful of your time.

I'm going to assume the timing isn't right and close out my follow-up for now.

If things change down the road — whether that's next month or next year — my door is always open. Just reply to this email and we'll pick up where we left off.

Wishing you the best with [their business/project].

[Your Name]

P.S. Sometimes this email gets a reply of "Actually, I've just been busy — let's talk!" If that's you, I'd love to hear from you. If not, no worries at all.

---

## Sequence Notes

**Timing:** Space emails 3–5 days apart. Don't send more than 2 per week.

**Personalization is key:** Each email should reference something specific to the lead's situation. Generic follow-ups get ignored. Use Prompt Chain #3 to customize.

**If they respond at any point:** Stop the sequence immediately. Switch to real conversation. Use Prompt Chain #6 (Response Handler) to craft your reply.

**Tracking:** Log each email sent in your lead tracker with the date and email number (e.g., "Nurture #3 sent 5/15").
